CompSoc CTF 2026 is done and I'm still buzzing from it.

In 2025, I helped out with the CTF as PRO. Cathal Lawlor ran that one and built the whole thing from the ground up. 93 registrations, 30+ teams, and it felt massive at the time. This year, as Auditor, I was the one organising it.

I'll be honest. I underestimated how much work it would take.

Organisation started later than it should have, and a lot of the prep ended up being last minute. We're all volunteers on the committee, so the actual hours each person could put in were limited. There's no getting around that. But the committee were incredible. They showed up when it mattered and pulled together to make the event what it was. I genuinely couldn't have done it without them.

On the day, 110+ students came from universities across Ireland. We'd overestimated attendance a bit, but we still grew from last year, and that's what counts. Teams were locked in from the start, working through challenges all day. Team ByteMe from UCC took first place. 2nd and 3rd were incredibly close.

We had four sponsors: Evernorth Health Services as platinum sponsor for the second year running (they were great to work with), Liberty IT, Centripetal, and Siren. Evernorth sent people down on the day too, which meant a lot. Stephen Walsh, their Head of HR in Ireland, gave us a shoutout afterwards.

The whole experience taught me a lot. Team coordination, financial planning and budgeting, sponsor interactions, event logistics. It's proper project management whether you call it that or not. Doing it as a volunteer society with limited time makes it even harder, but also more rewarding when it comes together.

CompSoc got shortlisted for the itag IT in the Community Award back in 2025, which felt like recognition that what we were building actually mattered outside the university. Growing the CTF further this year was a way of proving that wasn't a one-off.

I've since stepped down from Auditor to Treasurer. Wanted to try something different and pick up financial management experience. But CTF 2026 will probably be the thing I'm most proud of from my time in power.

Already thinking about CTF 2027.
