CompSoc CTF 2026 is finished, and I'm still buzzing from it.

## From helping to organising

In 2025, I helped with the CTF as PRO. Cathal Lawlor ran that event and built it from the ground up: 93 registrations, more than 30 teams, and something that felt massive at the time. This year, as Auditor, I was responsible for organising it.

I'll be honest: I underestimated how much work it would take.

Planning started later than it should have, so too much preparation became last-minute work. Everyone on the committee is a volunteer, and the hours people could contribute were limited. Still, they showed up when it mattered and pulled together to make the event happen. I genuinely could not have done it without them.

## The day itself

More than 110 students came from universities across Ireland. We had expected slightly more, but attendance still grew from the previous year. Teams were locked in from the start, working through challenges all day. Team ByteMe from UCC took first place, while second and third were incredibly close.

Four organisations sponsored the event: Evernorth Health Services returned as platinum sponsor for a second year, alongside Liberty IT, Centripetal, and Siren. Evernorth also sent representatives on the day, and Stephen Walsh, its Head of HR in Ireland, gave us a shout-out afterwards.

## What it taught me

The experience covered team coordination, budgeting, sponsor relationships, and event logistics. It is proper project management, whether or not a student society calls it that. Doing it with a volunteer team and limited time makes the work harder, but also much more rewarding when it comes together.

CompSoc was shortlisted for the itag IT in the Community Award in 2025. Growing the CTF again felt like evidence that the work recognised then was not a one-off.

I have since moved from Auditor to Treasurer to gain more financial-management experience. CTF 2026 will probably remain the thing I'm proudest of from my time on the CompSoc committee.

I'm already thinking about CTF 2027.
